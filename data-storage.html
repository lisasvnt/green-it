Understanding the environmental impact of data storage

What is Data Storage?

Data storage refers to the methods and technologies used to save digital information. Every email, photo, video, and document you store requires physical servers in data centers that consume energy 24/7.

The cloud isn't virtual ‚Äì it's made of millions of physical servers worldwide that need electricity, cooling, and regular hardware replacement.

üåç Environmental Impact

Energy Consumption: Data centers consume massive amounts of electricity for servers and cooling systems. Many still rely on fossil fuels, contributing to CO2 emissions.

Water Usage: Cooling systems in data centers consume millions of liters of water annually, putting pressure on local water resources.

E-waste: Servers need frequent replacement, generating tons of electronic waste containing toxic materials that can pollute ecosystems.

Land Use: Building data centers requires clearing land, potentially destroying natural habitats and affecting local biodiversity.
