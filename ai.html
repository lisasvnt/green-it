Understanding AI's environmental footprint

What is AI's Environmental Impact?

Artificial Intelligence systems require massive computational power for training and running models. From ChatGPT to image generators, every AI interaction consumes significant energy, with large language models requiring thousands of powerful GPUs running continuously.

While AI can help solve environmental problems, its own carbon footprint is growing rapidly and needs careful management.

üåç Environmental Impact

Training Phase: Training large AI models requires running thousands of GPUs for weeks or months, consuming massive amounts of electricity. This phase generates the most emissions.

Inference Phase: Every time you use AI (ask ChatGPT, generate an image), servers process your request. With billions of queries daily, this adds up significantly.

Cooling Requirements: AI servers generate intense heat, requiring powerful cooling systems that consume additional energy and water.

Hardware Waste: The race for more powerful AI drives demand for cutting-edge chips, leading to faster hardware obsolescence and e-waste.
